Edgar Allan Poe

Considered by many to be the father of the short story and the inventor of detective fiction, Poe was a master of the macabre. He is best known for his tales of terror, but he was also a poet. He became the topic of much scrutiny in 1835, when he married his 13-year-old cousin, but he is more often remembered for his publication a decade later.
