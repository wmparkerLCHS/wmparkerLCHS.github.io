J. R. R. Tolkien

Known mostly for his Lord of the Rings trilogy and other writings about Middle Earth, Tolkien wanted to give England her own mythology. He maintained that his work was never any kind of allegory; however many of his main themes are still very much relevant to today's society. Political and racial tensions, religious conflict, the drive for power, and the ongoing struggle between good and evil form the basis of Tolkien's epic and allow us to identify with his memorable characters. Perhaps most impressive is that Tolkien invented all of the geography and languages of Middle Earth.
